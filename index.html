Something is Wrong with the World

We are interested in perennial, deeply rooted historical conflicts and problems. We honour and value the spirit of free critical inquiry, the exercise of pure contemplative reason in the classical Hellenic sense, the questioning of all socially imposed world-views, and we cherish human rights, human dignity and human freedom. The genuine struggle against tyranny and superstition has nothing to do with uncritically adopting an equally debasing "scientific" mythology about the universe, the nature of life, human origins, the human mind and so forth. The critical, scientific spirit, the spirit of free inquiry and pure reason, the experimental method, have nothing to do with passively accepting narratives and dogmas or vague and ill-founded philosophical theories revolving around physicalism and empiricism. It is not so much about being concerned about certain opinions or behaviours but about the spirit and attitude with which such opinions are held and behaviours adopted: passive docility and social submission, anti-intellectualism and anti-individualism.

The philosophical position we defend is that of open ontological pluralism. But we focus on a logical, analytical approach to three irreducibly distinct realms of existence: the bio-physical world, the mental world of consciousness and the objective trans-mental logical-conceptual world. All three worlds are part of "nature" in the original philosophical meaning of this term. We totally agree with the "naturalist" rejection of the "supernatural" entities and events on empirical and logical grounds, without sharing in the least degree any untenable and self-defeating ontology or epistemology.

A chief task is a return to the spirit of Kant's Critique of Pure Reason through the subjection of discourse to formal logic elucidation, verification and analysis in the spirit of Frege and thereby finding the limits, incompleteness and underlying structure of all possible meaningful discourse.

We follow the attitude of Schopenhauer and Benatar in subjecting not only reason but the entirety of human existence to a systematic critique and admire a balanced and insightful attitude to religion: uncompromisingly honest in exposing its errors, absurdities and historical crimes and at the same time recognising therein valuable philosophical elements and deep positive tendencies in human nature at work.

On a more pragmatic level we are interested in solving the problem of human overpopulation and of all the suffering experienced by living beings on the earth today, specially with regards to man's treatment of animals. A special point is that those who do not experience such suffering are often very ungrateful for what they have and unaware of what others do not have, such as physical health, safety and enough to eat. Mankind has long since passed a healthy population limit, both in enconomical-environmental terms and relative to the value and dignity of individual life. We aim at combating different forms of pro-natalism and advocating a childfree life-style and adoption.
